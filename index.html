HCPSS Acting Superintendent

About Dr. Martirano

Dr. Michael J. Martirano was named acting superintendent for the Howard County Public School System in May 2017. He considers himself a teacher first and foremost and believes children must at the center of all decision-making. Guided by the principal that all children can and will learn, he is committed to ensuring equity and giving every child an opportunity to succeed. His ultimate goal is to improve student achievement and ensure a world-class education for all students.

Martirano most recently served as West Virginia state superintendent of schools, during which the state saw notable success in the areas of early childhood literacy, graduation rates and career technical education. Dr. Martirano previously served as superintendent of St. Mary's County Public Schools (Maryland), has been an educator for more than 30 years, and has a wide range of educational and administrative experience through his work as a science teacher, principal and visiting professor at Johns Hopkins University.
